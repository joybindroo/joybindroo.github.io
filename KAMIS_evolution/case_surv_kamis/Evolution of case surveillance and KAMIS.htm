Evolution of case surveillance and KAMIS

2008:

-          First challenges to the measurement of elimination

-          World Bank asked for a baseline assessment of proportion of blocks above elimination level

 

2009:

-          RMRI contracted to compare snowballing to the gold standard (HH survey) as a means of validating true incidence

 

2011:

-          Results showed that either method captured about 75% of cases that the other captured, but did not provide conclusive recommendations, finally published in 2016

 

2012:

-          TDR asked South Asian countries to develop a common format for reporting. NVBDCP took this up, and found wide variations in reporting formats and processes, particularly about village/block origin of cases

-          Work began to revise recording formats and reporting definitions for KAMIS

 

2013:

-          CARE took over task of strengthening KAMIS, including developing online information system

 

2013, 2015: Situation assessments:

-          How extensive is under-reporting of cases by the program? 7-17%

-          How accurate is the localization of cases? Gross misclassification of blocks with regard to incidence

-          How accurate are line-lists of cases? About 15% untraceable

 

2013-15: KAMIS introduced and established

-          Major reform: introduction of a nearly fool-proof method of establishing location of a case and cross-notification of non-resident cases

-          KAMIS offline and online field-tested, piloted in six districts and scaled up in Bihar and West Bengal

-          Major lacuna: village master-list not available, had to be created

-          NVBDCP/GoI only distantly involved, low belief in necessity and importance

 

2016:

-          NVBDCP/GoI became actively interested in KAMIS, asked for many changes, expecting elimination by 2017

-          Extensive clean-up operation undertaken by CARE teams to verify and clean up past line-lists - still an ongoing process

-          Active Case Detection begun in many districts by CARE teams, lessons accumulated

-          CARE formally started investigating case-clusters (- outbreaks- )

-          Attempted inclusion of geo-spatial information in KAMIS, using village and patient

 

2017:

-          Current model of Active Case Detection formally established all over Bihar and Jharkhand

-          Formal follow up of all cases instituted.

-          Online KAMIS rolled out in West Bengal.

 

2018:

-          Geotagging of all affected villages and cases taken up formally and nearly completed, in the process line-lists of past 6 years have become refined and more accurate

-          Detailed case profiles being maintained of all cases by CARE team.

-          KAMIS rolled-out in Uttar Pradesh

 

 

2019:

-          Mobile application of KAMIS launched for Android smartphones.

-          KAMIS designated as the main data tool for collection and organisation of KA program data by NVBDCP.

-          Use of inventory module for drug stock status reporting regularised.

-          Focus on completion of pending follow-ups of all cases. (CARE teams did at least one follow-up old cases)

-          Dashboards and reports section updated in KAMIS.

-          Efforts to port KAMIS on NIC servers started

 

2020:

-          Cyber security audit of KAMIS completed by recognised cyber security agency.

-          KAMIS software certified to be secure and safe for hosting for meeting compliance of NIC servers.

-          Multi-indicator dashboard updated with additional indicators.

-          Payment details entry in KAMIS streamlined.

-          Entry of suspect cases identified during active surveillance started by state.

-          Efforts to integrate entomology reports and data in KAMIS started
